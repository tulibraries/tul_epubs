Temple University Libraries

Charles, Interrupted

In August 2019, we celebrated the opening of the new Charles Library on Temple’s Main Campus. This new building boldly envisions the future of academic libraries, and is a powerful testament to Temple’s commitment to teaching and learning.

As we prepared our 2019 Annual Report, we were already looking forward to the 2020 issue, when we could share stories from our first year in Charles Library with you. But that first year was interrupted in March 2020, when Temple moved classes fully online in an effort to keep the Temple community safe and to slow the spread of COVID-19. Charles Library, along with all our other library locations, was closed to the public until August of this year.

Despite these very challenging circumstances, we remained committed to serving our patrons and providing important

resources and services even as our physical locations were closed. Library staff worked quickly to offer expanded access to online journals and databases, moved workshops and reference consultations online, contributed to a universitywide taskforce to produce PPE for Temple University Hospital, created tutorials and other online learning resources, and more.

As you read this report, Charles Library is open once more. We have enacted a variety of measures to ensure the safety of our visitors and staff at Charles and all our libraries, and we will continue to adapt and persevere in the coming months. In the meantime, we are grateful for this opportunity to share with you a few of our favorite memories from this first year in Charles and to celebrate some of the spaces, features, and people that make it such a special place.

The First Year in Charles Library

A grand opening

We hosted a formal dedication ceremony for Charles Library on September 12, 2019. Representatives from the city and state were in attendance, along with university leaders, trustees, donors, architects, representatives from the student body, and the library’s namesake Steve Charles, KLN ‘80.

The following week, we held a massive all-campus celebration and welcomed students, faculty, staff, and members of the community into the new space. The day was filled with musical performances, 3D printing and VR demonstrations in the Loretta C. Duckworth Scholars Studio, a poetry reading by alumna Yolanda Wisher, and informal presentations about our services and other library locations.
