Annual Report 2020

Mary Rose Muccie, executive director/scholarly communications officer for Temple University Press, served on the program committee and acted as a facilitator for the P2L4 (a community of university presses and research libraries with whom they have a reporting relationship) conference.

Vitalina Nova, education and community engagement librarian, co-authored an article on trauma-informed pedagogy including context and suggestions for information literacy instructors in Reference Services Review special issue, Academic Libraries and the 45th President.

Charlie Perkins, head of public services at the Beasley School of Law Library, provided a book review about Foundations of Information Ethics for the Law Library Journal and wrote a short article “Deepfakes, Cheap Fakes, and the Information Professional” for The CRIV Sheet: The Newsletter of the Committee on Relations with Information Vendors.

Jenny Pierce, head of the science, technology, engineering, and biomedical Learning and Research Services unit, was interviewed for and is quoted in an article, “How Medical Librarians Are Handling the Coronavirus Crisis,” for Library Journal.

Julie Randolph, reference librarian at the Beasley School of Law, is serving on the Board of Directors of Greater Philadelphia Law Library Association and chairs its Bylaws Committee and also served on AALL’s Legal Research Competencies Review Special Committee.

Katy Rawdon, SCRC coordinator of technical services, served on the Finance Committee of the Mid-Atlantic Regional Archives Conference (MARAC).

Stephanie Roth, biomedical and research services librarian, was an invited speaker for the NISO 2020 virtual conference entitled Researcher Behaviors and the Impact of Technology. Her presentation topic was “Tools to Support Systematic Review Research.”

Darryl Sanford, bibliographic assistant, served as a member of the Ambler Campus Sustainability Council.

Emily Schiller, bibliographic assistant II, participated in a panel titled “Stepping Forward: Temple’s Community Programs” at the Philadelphia Technology Learning Collaborative 2019 Annual Conference held at the Free Library of Philadelphia in November.

Caitlin Shanley, librarian and coordinator of Learning and Student Success, was a panelist at the NCOW Community Forum during Temple University’s National Coming Out Week in October 2019. Caitlin served as co-chair of the ACRL Women and Gender Studies Section Instruction Committee, and was elected Member-at-Large of the Section.

Margery Sly, SCRC director, served as the vice president of the Society of American Archivists’ Foundation and as a member of the SAA Dictionary of Archives Terminology Working Group.

Gretchen Sneff, science and engineering librarian, co-authored a data primer, Confocal Microscopy Data Curation Primer, with co-authors Susan Ivey, Amy Koshoffer, and Huajin Wang.

Natalie Tagge, head of the Podiatry Library, was invited to serve a three-year term on the Editorial Board of the Journal of the Medical Library Association.

Holly Tomren, head of Metadata and Digitization Services, co-authored “Creating Library Linked Data with Wikibase: Lessons Learned from Project Passage” along with Jean Godby, Karen Smith-Yoshimura, Bruce Washburn, Kalan Davis, Karen Detling, Christine Fernsebner Eslao, Steven Folsom, Xiaoli Li, Marc McGee, Karen Miller, Honor Moody, and Craig Thomas in OCLC Research.

Kimberly Tully, curator of rare books, served the Rare Books and Manuscripts Section of Association of College and Research Libraries (RBMS/ACRL) as a member of the Budget and Development Committee and as a member of the Scholarships Committee.

Diane Turner, curator of the Charles L. Blockson Afro-American Collection, participated in a book signing of Philadelphia Jazz Stories Illustrated: Volume One, which includes an essay and interviews by her, at the Barnes Foundation.

Sara Wilson, assistant director of library outreach and communications, wrote a column, “Goodbye, Paley… Hello, Charles!: Marketing a Library Move,” for Public Services Quarterly.
