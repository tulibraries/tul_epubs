Annual Report 2020

To increase support of the Temple Health Sciences Center patrons, Health Sciences Libraries staff moved workshops online at the start of the pandemic. They also scheduled new workshops in increasing numbers. From March 17th to June 30th, they held 20 workshops with 320 registrants from across Temple, the region, and the world, including Temple alumni in California and students from Portugal.

Attendees enjoyed the opportunity to attend remotely and found the sessions engaging and well produced. Comments included:

“Great online transition—really I had no idea it was the first online presentation of this workshop since it ran so smoothly!”

“Zoom was an effective method to present this information. It went very smoothly and the polls were effective. I think that this is a good method for presentation in the future and would allow for larger groups to sit in on the information.”

Doctoral students in the Music Therapy Department are expected to know systematic review skills as part of their preparation for their dissertations. After Stephanie Roth, biomedical and research services librarian, assisted a student in understanding these skills, Dr. Wendy Magee sent this email to Stephanie: “As for the type of help you have provided, it is exactly health sciences help that the students need for these reviews. [...] So your specific skills for this class and level of work is really needed.”

The Law Library began a collaboration with Temple Law’s International Programs to provide resources to current and future students, as well as program alumni. The law librarians created a research guide geared towards international students’ needs and produced a series of videos on topics such as United States courts and government, taking the bar exam, and using legal databases.

She did some of her research for “‘Don’t Buy Where You Can’t Work’: Leon Sullivan’s Fight Against Job Discrimination” in the Libraries’ Special Collections Research Center.

Annual Report 2020

Natalie Tagge, head of the Podiatry Library, always tells attendees at the “Best Practices in Poster Presentation” workshop that they can email her after the workshop with a poster for her to review. A student emailed Natalie asking if she would review her first-ever poster presentation that she would be submitting in two weeks, and Natalie provided feedback based on the content of the workshop.

A third year School of Podiatric Medicine student experimented with the Podiatry Library’s 3D printer to make 3D models of arteries to help first year students learn about and visualize nerves and blood vessels.
