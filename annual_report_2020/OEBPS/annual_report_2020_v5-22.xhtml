Temple University Libraries

Lisa Fitch, senior associate director of PhD programs in the Office of Research and Doctoral Programs at the Fox School of Business, reached out to Curator of the Blockson Collection, Diane Turner, after an event to say:

“A tremendous thank you for allowing FOX to use the Feinstone Lounge and the Charles L. Blockson Afro-American Collection for the Fox School Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion Networking Event yesterday. The space was beautiful and suitable for the celebration of Black History Month and Temple University’s history of supporting diversity, equity, and inclusion. The exhibitions were well received by the guests and I hope that our faculty, staff, and students who attended the event will encourage others to visit the Collection. Also, special thanks to Aslaku Berhanu and your staff for their assistance in providing materials and information to us during the event.”

While campus was closed, School of Social Work students working on literature reviews met with the social work librarian, Olivia Given Castello, via Zoom for advice on their research strategies for finding intervention studies on trauma treatment effectiveness, and tips on finding the full text of articles. One student wrote, “I only wish I had gone to her BEFORE I started my paper.”

Library Stories

A student needed help getting started on finding published research in the Speech, Language, and Hearing Sciences. The librarian staffing Temple Libraries’ chat service, Sarah Jones, directed them to the libraries’ Communication Sciences and Disorders guide, which was exactly what they needed to jumpstart their research. The student wrote that the chat experience “couldn’t have been better. [The librarian] quickly and efficiently directed me to my intended resource with no delay. Much appreciated.”

A recent College of Engineering alum was able to get a quick answer to their eresources question from the librarian staffing Temple Libraries’ chat service, Andrew Diamond. The patron later wrote, “for me, as [a] recent alum, the library resources have been valuable as a resource for entrepreneurship and career development.” Their follow-up feedback was also glowing, “You can’t improve on perfection!”

The Innovation Space at Ginsburg Health Sciences Library continued to be a popular resource for teaching and learning. For example, Dr. Hillel Maresky (Radiology) taught residents cardiovascular anatomy with the Sharecare VR program in the Innovation Space, while Dr. John Duffy brought nursing students to the space to use the Empathy Training Software. And in November, the Innovation Space was a featured stop on tours Dr. John Daly, Lewis Katz School of Medicine (LKSOM) interim dean and dean emeritus, led for the Chairman of the Board of Trustees, Mitchell Morgan, and Executive Vice President and Provost, JoAnne Epps.
