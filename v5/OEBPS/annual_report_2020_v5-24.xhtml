Temple University Libraries

Staff Accomplishments

Recent Presentations, Publications, and Professional Service

Aslaku Berhanu, Charles L. Blockson Afro-American Collection librarian, completed work on an Institute for Museum and Library Services (IMLS) grant to conserve rare books in the Blockson Collection. She also gave an interview to the Urban Institute Center, a national nonprofit research organization, about her work on the grant.

Charles L. Blockson, curator emeritus of the Charles L. Blockson Afro-American Collection, was presented two awards in October 2019. The first was the 2019 Dr. Dorothy Porter Wesley Award for Excellence in contributing to the Information Profession. This was awarded by the Association for the Study of African American Life and History and presented to Mr. Blockson by Temple University President, Richard Englert. The second award, presented by radio personality W. Cody Anderson, was from the Masons.

Olivia Given Castello, head of the business, social sciences, and education Learning and Research Services unit, with collaborators Lauri Friedman from the Temple University School of Social Work and Joel Nichols from the Free Library of Philadelphia (FLP), received an ARL Venture Fund grant to sponsor trauma-informed services training for FLP and Temple Libraries staff. With fellow members of the Education & Behavioral Science Section’s (EBSS) Social Work committee, she co-presented an ACRL webinar Connecting Justice to Frameworks: Information Literacy in Social Work.

Michelle Cosby, director of the Law Library, was honored as one of the Fastcase 50, which recognizes leaders, visionaries, and innovators in the legal field. She also testified before the House Appropriations Subcommittee on the Legislative Branch in Washington, D.C. about funding for the United States Government Publishing Office and the Library of Congress and served as president of the American Association of Law Libraries, presiding over the Association’s first-ever virtual annual meeting.

Will Dean, research and data services librarian, presented a lightning talk about using his position as a REDCap administrator to effectively connect library services to our research community at the Empirical Librarians conference in February 2020.

Staff News

Nicole DeSarno, instructional designer, and Olivia Given Castello, head of the business, social sciences, and education Learning and Research Services unit, co-presented a poster at the Center for the Advancement of Teaching’s 18th Annual Faculty Conference on Teaching Excellence entitled Reaching Them Where They Are: Put the Library Where Students are Learning with LMS Integration. It won 3rd place in the poster competition.

Kristina De Voe, English and communication librarian, presented “Intersections of Critical Information Literacy and Open Pedagogy: Zines and Wikipedia in the Classroom” at the 2020 Georgia International Conference on Information Literacy in Savannah, GA.

Courtney Eger, learning and engagement librarian, was selected to join the editorial board of the open access publication, Marketing Libraries Journal, and serves as a proofreader.

Andrea Goldstein, librarian, served on the universitywide Financial Literacy Committee.

Josué Hurtado, librarian and coordinator of public services in the SCRC, served as the founding chair of the Mid-Atlantic Regional Archives Conference’s Diversity and Inclusion Committee.

Aaron Javsicas, editor-in-chief of Temple University Press, spoke about book publishing as a career to English Studies master’s students in Rutgers Camden Professor Holly Blackford’s Proseminar.

Latanya Jenkins, government information librarian, was granted a summer 2020 stipend for the Teaching the Global African Diaspora one-day workshop for world history teachers from the University of Pittsburgh’s Alliance for Learning in World History. Latanya was also accepted into the summer/fall 2020 Library Freedom Institute.

Sarah Jones and Gretchen Sneff, science and engineering librarians, co-presented “Are Your Students Ready to Create Open Data?” at OpenCon Philly Philadelphia, PA in November 2019.
