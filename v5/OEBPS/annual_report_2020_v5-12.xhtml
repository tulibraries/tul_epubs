Temple University Libraries

Library staff worked with the College of Public Health to help publish the first issue of their open access online journal, CommonHealth. A peer-reviewed journal, CommonHealth publishes papers of interest to public health scholars. The Libraries are hosting the journal via an open source platform, and all articles are immediately freely available to read, download, and share. This collaborative project showcases the groundbreaking work being conducted by our Temple students and faculty.

The Libraries launched TUScholarShare, the university’s digital archive for compiling, storing, and sharing the wide range of scholarly works created in the course of research and teaching at Temple. The repository makes Temple scholarship freely available online to a global audience, with the goal of advancing knowledge and learning.

In the same month that we opened Charles Library, we also launched a brand new library website after several years of development and user testing. It serves to enhance discovery of library resources, and to promote the use of services and amenities both online and within our walls.

As we celebrated the 100th anniversary of women’s right to vote this year, staff from the SCRC and Metadata and Digitization Services continued to work on “In Her Own Right: A Century of Women’s Activism, 1820-1920,” a project of the Philadelphia Area Consortium of Special Collections Libraries (PACSCL). “In Her Own Right” is a multi-phase, multi-grant-funded project to digitize and make accessible primary sources documenting women working for women’s rights in the century leading up to women getting the vote in Philadelphia and beyond.
