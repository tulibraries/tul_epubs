Temple University Libraries

New Staff

Jim Boyce is the new Charles Library evening building supervisor. Jim has worked in various facilities management roles, most recently as a member of the groundskeeping department at Temple University. He is currently pursuing a bachelor of arts at the Tyler School of Art and Architecture in the Facilities Management program.

Lara Davidson is the new bibliographic assistant II in the Access Services department. She joins us from the Schoenberg Center for Electronic Text and Images at the University of Pennsylvania Libraries. Lara graduated from Maryland Institute College of Art with a BFA in photography with a concentration in sustainability and social practice.

Christopher Green is the new technical support specialist for Library Technology Services. He comes to us from the City of Philadelphia where he worked in the Office of Innovation & Technology and the Office of Property Assessment.

Geneva Heffernan is the new lead administrative specialist on the Library Outreach and Communications team. Geneva recently graduated from Klein College with a BA in Journalism and Economics. Previously, she interned for the Philadelphia Inquirer and worked with the Philadelphia Phillies as a marketing photographer.

Brian Hoffman is the new senior developer for the Library Technology Development team. Prior to joining Temple, Brian worked as a developer consultant on numerous library and archives projects, including OJS (Open Journals System), the IMLS funded “Saving Data Journalism” project, Harvard’s LibraryCloud system, and ArchivesSpace. Previously, he worked at NYU Libraries and Columbia University.

Rolando Portocarrero is the new evening bibliographic assistant II in the Access Services department. While attending Temple, Rolando worked as a circulation student worker in Paley Library.

Shaun Vigil is the new Temple University Press acquiring editor for the humanities. He brings a wealth of experience to the Press, including stints at Palgrave Macmillan, Cambridge University Press, and Columbia University Press.
